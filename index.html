The Numbers

What a good life in Bangkok costs per month.

The table covers most of it. The number that tends to surprise people isn't the rent — it's healthcare. A solid expat health insurance plan runs $80 to $120 a month. A GP visit costs around $25. Private hospitals in Bangkok are genuinely good.

Dental care isn't covered by Medicare at home. A cleaning at a reputable private clinic in Bangkok costs about $35.

Thailand is affordable. It is not free. The retirement visa income threshold exists for a reason, and anyone who arrives without sufficient funds runs into serious problems. That conversation happens on the free call, before anyone books a flight.

The visa threshold is real. It exists for a reason.

Thailand's retirement visa requires you to be 50 years of age or older, with either 800,000 THB in a Thai bank account (about $22,000 USD) or roughly $2,200/month in guaranteed income. If you're under 50, this specific pathway isn't available yet. If the finances aren't quite there, that conversation happens on the free call — before anyone books a flight.
